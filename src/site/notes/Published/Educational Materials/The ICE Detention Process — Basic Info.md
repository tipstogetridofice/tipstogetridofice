### Immediately Following Detention 
- What happens? 
	- US citizens are typically processed separately from those of different immigration status â€”Â most often, they are **released within a few hours.** Calling the NLG hotline is still recommended: **612-444-2654** ([source](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1EnacBDU1KBefoBekQRNvENwg3BdLVT12me1m7ZgEQOY/edit?tab=t.0))

([source](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1EnacBDU1KBefoBekQRNvENwg3BdLVT12me1m7ZgEQOY/edit?tab=t.0))
- **In Minnesota** 
	- **Call the NLG Hotline 612-444-2654**
	- If someone has just been detained by ICE and has not yet been transferred to Whipple or out of Minnesota, there is a narrow window to act. **Whether a family member, Next Friend, or attorney is contacted first, the following information should be gathered as quickly as possible to allow immediate habeas filing ([source](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1EnacBDU1KBefoBekQRNvENwg3BdLVT12me1m7ZgEQOY/edit?tab=t.0)):**
		- Full legal name
		- Date of birth
		- Alien number (A-Number), if known
		- Immigration history (especially confirmation of no final removal order)
		- City of residence in Minnesota
		- Location, date, and time of arrest
		- Current detention location (if known) â€” for example, local holding facility, Whipple, or ICE processing site
		- Phone number of a family member or trusted contact
		- Name and status (U.S. citizen or LPR) of the Next Friend
	- This information **allows a habeas petition to be filed immediately,** even if an attorney is not yet involved. If the A-Number or detention location is unknown, the petition can still be filed, but families should continue trying to obtain that information as soon as possible ([source](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1EnacBDU1KBefoBekQRNvENwg3BdLVT12me1m7ZgEQOY/edit?tab=t.0)).
