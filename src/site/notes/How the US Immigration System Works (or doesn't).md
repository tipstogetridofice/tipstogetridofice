# How US Immigration Works
I confess that had little understanding of the US immigration system prior to starting this project and research. **While a comprehensive understanding is not required in order to help our communities against the threat of ICE, I am detailing what I have learned here as a resource for those who have questions.** 

***Knowledge is power, although it is no substitute for action.*** 
## Refugees & Refugee Status 

> [!Important Advice: Do Not Open the Door]
> ICE Officers require a judicial warrant, signed by a judge, in order to enter your property without your consent. It's important to [know your rights](https://support.iraplegalinfo.org/hc/en-us/articles/43974720166420-I-live-in-the-US-and-I-have-refugee-status-What-should-I-know#h_01KEWPBJYDCE7PB68C7606TZ19) in case ICE comes to your home ([Source: IRAP](https://support.iraplegalinfo.org/hc/en-us/articles/43974720166420-I-live-in-the-US-and-I-have-refugee-status-What-should-I-know)). Visit [this webpage](https://support.iraplegalinfo.org/hc/en-us/articles/43974720166420-I-live-in-the-US-and-I-have-refugee-status-What-should-I-know) and scroll down to see an example of a Judicial Warrant. ICE may show you an administrative arrest warrant or immigration detainer. **If it is not a judicial warrant, you do not have to let them into your home.**

### What is Refugee Status? 
[source: IRAP](https://refugeerights.org/news-resources/u-h-a-v-bondi-challenging-the-trump-administrations-detention-of-refugees-in-minnesota)
**Remember: If you have refugee status, you have legal permission to be in the United States. If stopped or targeted by ICE, remain calm.**
- A refugee (according to US law) is (1) outside their country, (2) unwilling or unable to return because they have been badly harmed or fear being badly harmed due to their "race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership in a particular social group," and/or who meets various other specific legal requirements. 
- The UN also grants refugee status, but this does not guarantee you refugee status in the US, and US law does not necessarily apply to you if you only have UNHCR refugee status or refugee status from another country. 
- Refugee status is **not** the same thing as asylum â€”Â people who apply for immigration status from within the US are called **aslyees**. 
- People with refugee status will have one or more of the following documents indicating their status: 
	- Transportation boarding letter (brought when they entered the US by themself or the 'principle applicant' with whom they are associated) 
	- V93 Boarding Foil (if they are the follow-to-join spouse or child of a refugee who arrived before them) 
	- EAD (work permit with the category (a)(3))
	- I-94 (arrival/departure record, aka proof of legal entry into the country. Refugees will have the Class of Admission 'RE'. This form can be found on [this website](https://i94.cbp.dhs.gov/search/recent-search))
	- **If you don't have/can't find the above any/all of the above documents, contact an immigration attorney to ensure that you have sufficient proof of your refugee status.**
- **Refugee status does NOT expire, and it does not need to be renewed.**
	- However, if a refugee is convicted of certain crimes or has been accused of using fake documents, they may be placed in "removal proceedings," which can result in deportation. **Have an immigration attorney on hand.** 
	- **ICE may use lies or trickery regarding refugee status and procedures in order to detain you. Learn more about [[ICE's Current Tactics\|ICE's Current Tactics]].** 
- Refugees hold certain benefits such as the right to work in the US, access to benefits such as food and housing, and the right to apply for lawful permanent residence. 
- Refugees can travel abroad, but must obtain a Refugee Travel Document (RTD) before leaving (Source and More Info from [the IRAP](https://support.iraplegalinfo.org/hc/en-us/articles/360056581372-I-have-refugee-or-approved-asylum-status-and-I-want-to-travel-outside-of-the-U-S)).
